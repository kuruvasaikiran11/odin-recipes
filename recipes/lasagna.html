Lasagna

Lasagna is a type of pasta, possibly one of the oldest types, made of very wide, flat sheets.
Either term can also refer to an Italian dish made of stacked layers of lasagna alternating with fillings such as ragù, vegetables, cheeses (which may include ricotta, mozzarella, and parmesan), and seasonings and spices.

Ingredients

  • 1 to 1-1/2lb ground meat, depending on preference
  • 1lb (0.45 kilograms) ricotta cheese
  • 1lb (0.45 kilograms) shredded Mozzarella cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 box Lasagna noodles
  • 2 cups of cheddar cheese

Steps

  1. Cook the noodles in boiling water
  2. Add a diced medium white onion and 2 cloves of chopped garlic and cook for 2-3 minutes until they start becoming translucent
  3. Transfer the meat and vegetables to a large sauce pot on medium-low heat
  4. Cook the sauce on a low simmer for 10-15 minutes
  5. Lay lasagna noodles over the entire bottom of the dish
  6. Bake for 30-40 minutes at 375℉, covered with aluminum foil
  7. Let the lasagna sit for 10 minutes before serving
